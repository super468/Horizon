## [Quantization Explained: Compressing LLMs for Consumer GPUs](https://ngrok.com/blog/quantization) ⭐️ 8.0/10

An educational article explains quantization—the process of compressing neural network weights from 32-bit floats to 4-bit integers—demonstrating how Q4_K_M quantization reduces a 27B parameter model from 54GB to 16GB of VRAM, enabling it to run on consumer hardware like the RTX 3090. This matters because quantization democratizes access to large language models. Without quantization, running a 27B model requires professional-grade hardware with 50+ GB of VRAM; with Q4 quantization, anyone with a used GPU around $900 can run such models locally. The article uses a float comparison slider for visual learning. Key quantization formats include Q2_K, Q4_K_M, Q6_K, and Q8_0, with lower bit depths offering smaller model sizes at the cost of accuracy. The 'K_M' suffix indicates k-means quantization at the optimal size.

hackernews · Lobsters - AI · Mar 25, 16:06

**Background**: Quantization reduces the precision of neural network weights from 32-bit floating point (FP32) to lower-precision integers (e.g., 4-bit or 8-bit). This compressed representation is stored and loaded from memory, then dequantized on-the-fly during inference. The main frameworks for LLM quantization are GGUF, GPTQ, and AWQ.
