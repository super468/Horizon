## [Mirror Bacteria Biosecurity Risk Debated at NSF Workshop](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/04/15/1135197/synthetic-mirror-life-microbes-kill-us-all/) ⭐️ 8.0/10

In February 2019, about 30 synthetic biologists and ethicists met at an NSF-funded workshop in Northern Virginia to discuss whether the NSF should fund research into creating "mirror bacteria" — synthetic microorganisms built from molecules with reversed chirality that could pose existential biosecurity risks. If mirror bacteria were created, they could interact unpredictably with Earth's ecosystems, potentially outcompeting natural organisms or causing catastrophic ecological disruption. Some experts believe this represents one of the most severe emerging biosecurity threats, potentially more dangerous than known pathogens. Mirror bacteria would be built from enantiomers — molecules that are non-superimposable mirror images of naturally occurring biological molecules. Researchers estimate it may take 10-30 years to actually create mirror bacteria, though the field advances rapidly. The workshop specifically discussed whether this research should receive NSF funding.

rss · MIT Technology Review · Apr 15, 09:00

**Background**: Chirality refers to the property of molecules that cannot be superimposed on their mirror image, like left and right hands. All naturally occurring life uses only left-handed amino acids and right-handed sugars. Mirror life would use the opposite versions, making it fundamentally different from any biological system on Earth. This could theoretically allow mirror bacteria to evade immune systems and outcompete normal organisms in unexpected ways.
