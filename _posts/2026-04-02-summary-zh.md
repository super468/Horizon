The Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures (CVE) system provides standardized identifiers for publicly known security vulnerabilities. Kernel-level vulnerabilities are particularly critical because they operate with the highest privilege level, and modern operating systems implement protections like KASLR and stack canaries to make exploitation more difficult. FreeBSD is a Unix-like operating system known for its advanced networking and security features.
